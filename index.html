FAQ

Common questions

What is a fiduciary?

A fiduciary is legally required to act in your best interest at all times. Not all financial advisors are fiduciaries. Many operate under a lower standard that only requires their recommendations be suitable, not necessarily best for you. As a fiduciary we are always on your side.

What does fee-only mean?

Fee-only means we are compensated solely by you, our client. We do not earn commissions, referral fees, or any compensation from third parties. This eliminates conflicts of interest and ensures our advice is always objective.

Do I need a minimum amount to get started?

We generally seek relationships with clients who have at least $100,000 in investable assets, though we evaluate each situation individually. We also offer financial planning on a flat fee or hourly basis for clients who are still building toward that level.

How do you charge?

We offer three fee structures depending on your situation. A 1% AUM fee for clients who want full investment management, a $200 monthly retainer for clients building wealth who want ongoing planning and access, and project based pricing at $250 per hour or $1,500 for a comprehensive plan. All fees are disclosed upfront.

Can I work with you if I am not in North Carolina?

Yes. We serve clients both in person in NC and virtually across the country. Most of our planning and review meetings can be conducted via video call, making it easy to work together regardless of where you are located.

What makes Hammond Wealth Partners different?

Most large firms assign you to an advisor who manages hundreds of clients and follows a standardized playbook. At Hammond Wealth Partners you work directly with Ryan Hammond, an economics-trained advisor who takes a macro-driven, actively engaged approach to your financial plan. No minimums. No commissions. No autopilot.
